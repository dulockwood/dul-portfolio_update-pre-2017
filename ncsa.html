Initial Thumbnail Sketches

Immediately after the initial contact with the client, the creative juices started flowing. I didn't let inspiration go wasted, knowing I'd likely begin working under contract soon. A rebranding consists of many things, but a strong logo brings all the parts together.

Some thumbnail concepts deviated strongly from the direction of repurposing their original lighthouse logo. To give the NCSA its own character in comparison to the other state societies of anesthesiologists, I determined the best direction was to keep the lighthouse (iconic of NC) to fit with the society's slogan: "The Beacon of Patient Safety in North Carolina."

First Logo Iteration

One thumbnail sketch in particular stood out, due to its balance and potential development in the refinement process. I brought a scan of the thumbnail into Illustrator and made a rough, mathematically-sound manifestation of the concept.

Planning the Next Iteration

After seeing the potential of the first iteration, I went back to the sketchbook to make adjustments and create a better base to design from.

Second Logo Iteration

In the same process as with the first iteration, I took the sketch into Illustrator for refinement. This second iteration felt much stronger than the first, but needed further detail to better reflect the society's slogan.

Multiple Options

Numerous slight edits in order to provide the client with several viable options.

Brand Testing

With the information already given by the client, I've begun to form a basis for how I believe the site should look. Work will proceed when I have received the specific content the client wants displayed.
