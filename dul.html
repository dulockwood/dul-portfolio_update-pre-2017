DUL Perspective

UPCOMING YOUTUBE CHANNEL
MARCH 2017 - ???

I am in the process of creating a YouTube channel to serve as an outlet for my thoughts on political and cultural events. DUL Perspective will aim to educate viewers on a variety of politically-relevant topics, preserving a clear separation between objective fact and my personal opinion. I hope to foster rational discussion on even the most heated topics, and potentially have my own views challenged.

Intro Bump [Current Draft]

I believe first impressions are critical to the potential success of any professional endeavor. If I want to be taken seriously, then I need to put in the effort to look the part.

While I'm excited with the results of my work on this introductory animation thus far, there is clearly work to be done before it is ready. I intend to finesse the timing of elements and motion prior to releasing my first video.

Evocative Thumbnails

Nearly as important for first impressions are a channel's utilization of video thumbnails. Good thumbnails get clicks. Consistency in design and branding across thumbnails alert a viewer to more content from the same source.

This is the current draft of my channel's thumbnail design. Rarely will the thumbnail contain the actual title of the video. Instead, it will supplement that title or draw attention to an important quote discussed in the video.

Keeping It All In Perspective

To arrive at DUL Perspective's branding, I drew inspiration from the philosophical reflections of influential scientific figures. In particular, Carl Sagan's discussion about the Pale Blue Dot image heavily influenced the decision to feature the Earth as a minor element in a larger system.

No Sense In Hiding Opinion

With the immediacy of the introduction, I present an opinion.

"We Are Here" and "We Need To Be Here," referring to Earth and Mars respectively, illustrate the idea that humanity must prioritize achieving habitability of other worlds.

It is nearly impossible to achieve pure objectivity in journalism. Even the subtlety of word choice can reveal the inherent biases of an author. I opt to share my beliefs in hopes of allowing a dissenting viewer to look past a strawman and see nuance.

Straightforward, No-Nonsense Type

The pairing of Serif Sentinel and Sans-Serif Gotham echoes the usual personality of journalistic publications.

A Palette For Any Situation

While DUL Perspective primarily utilizes grayscale for most elements, I chose 6 bold accent colors across the spectrum for a range of potential use cases.
