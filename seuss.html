Red State, Blue State, Rich State, Poor State

UNC SCHOOL OF MEDIA AND JOURNALISM
JUNE 2013 + FEBRUARY 2015

Statistician and political scientist, Andrew Gelman, wrote an engaging analysis on the political landscape of America, focusing on the emerging trend of dividing the electorate into color-coded states. The clever title he chose for his book, Red State, Blue State, Rich State, Poor State, references a work of early-childhood author, Dr. Seuss.

A class project calling for the redesign of a non-fiction book created the perfect opportunity to give Gelman's book a fitting cover. Drawing inspiration from the art style of Dr. Seuss, I created caricatures of then-Speaker of the House Nancy Pelosi, and then-House Minority Leader John Boehner.

Current Version

I revisited the project in 2015 and made improvements to the typography, color and detail of the piece—as a way to see how much I had progressed during my time as a college student.

Original Version

This was my first major project utilizing only vector illustration. I remember being proud of what I had produced, although I can look back and reflect on my weaknesses. I hope to always to improve my skillset and have a critical, retrospective eye for past designs.
