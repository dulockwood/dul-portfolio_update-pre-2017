A Future To Believe In

BERNIE SANDERS FOR PRESIDENT
JANUARY 2016 - MAY 2016

I had the privilege of utilizing my design skills while working directly with the national campaign during the 2016 Democratic Primary. I created both digital and print media with turnaround times no longer than a few hours.

NC Social Media Avatar

The campaign promoted a social media competition to encourage support for Bernie and remind voters of the upcoming Democratic primaries.

Voters were able to change their profile pictures to one of several avatars, which I created under the direction of the national campaign.

Charlotte, NC Rally Flyer

Volunteers handed these half-sheet flyers out by the thousands during the March 14th rally.

State-specific variants were used at other rallies to spread awareness of each state's social media competition.

The Young Revolution

CAROLINA STUDENTS FOR BERNIE SANDERS
SEPTEMBER 2015 - JANUARY 2016

Carolina Students for Bernie is the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill branch of the Bernie Sanders presidential campaign. I joined the newly-formed organization in September 2015, utilizing an opportunity to develop their brand and logo as well as gain experience managing other designers. CSFB acted as the ground game for the Chapel Hill area, encouraging students, faculty, and city residents to participate in the democratic process.

Organization Logo

The logo combined the graphic elements of UNC's Old Well logo and the typography of the Bernie Sanders' campaign. This showed our solidarity with the campaign as well as reinforced our focus on the city of Chapel Hill. The left side of the logo was designed to stand alone as an icon for alternative purposes.

Rally Against Student Debt Flyer

This rally, hosted by the North Carolina College Students for Bernie, sought to organize Americans dissatisfied with the systemic problem of massive tuition costs to attend college.

Handed out by volunteers to North Carolinian residents, these flyers directed supporters to the precise location of the rally using the familiar graphic elements of Google Maps.

"The Pit" Campaign Flyers

These half-sheet flyers—printed en masse in black-and-white and handed out to students passing through "The Pit," a centralized location on campus—spread awareness of the campaign and our involvement.

The first flyer informs potential NC voters of the early voting period and the new Voter ID law in the state.

The second flyer visualizes the history of the minimum wage and argues the merits for raising it.

Tabasco Bern Sauce

Bernie's campaign utilized social media to energize a millennial base and dominate online discussion. The Twitter hashtag, #feelthebern, quickly grew in popularity and became the main hashtag to represent the anti-establishment progressive movement during the campaign. This photoshopped Tabasco sauce bottle was my small contribution to the phenomenon.
